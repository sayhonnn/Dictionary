k [misc] the eleventh letter of the English alphabet is nonvocal consonant. The form and sound of the letter K are from the Latin which used the letter but little except in the early period of the language. It came into the Latin from the Greek which received it from a Phoenician source the ultimate origin probably being Egyptian. Etymologically K is most nearly related to c g h (which see).
