y- [misc] A prefix of obscure meaning originally used with verbs adverbs adjectives nouns and pronouns. In the Middle English period it was little employed except with verbs being chiefly used with past participles though occasionally with the infinitive. The movement has successfully expanded not only among young men in general but also specifically among railroad men in the army and navy with provision for Indians and negroes and a full duplication of all the various lines of oepration in the boys' departments.
